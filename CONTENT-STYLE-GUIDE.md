# Poddar Wealth — Content Style Guide

This is the editorial standard for ALL content on the site.
Both agents and humans reference this for every blog post, service page,
and any body text longer than 2 paragraphs.

---

## Voice

Write like Ajay Kumar Poddar: a trusted advisor with 31 years of experience
who explains things clearly and cares about your family. Not a salesman.
Not a chatbot. A real person who picks up the phone.

---

## Punctuation & Structure

1. Never use em-dashes (—). Use commas, periods, or new sentences.
2. Use contractions (it's, don't, you're, won't, can't, that's).
3. Mix sentence lengths. Short sentences hit harder.
4. Write in paragraphs, not bullet lists. If you must list, use 3 items max inline ("things like X, Y, and Z").
5. No more than 3 sentences per paragraph for readability.

---

## Forbidden Words (never use)

Delve, testament, tapestry, ultimate, leverage, pivot, seamlessly,
resonate, elevated, game-changer, landscape, furthermore, moreover,
look no further, in conclusion, it goes without saying, needless to say,
at the end of the day, in today's world, comprehensive, cutting-edge,
navigating, empower, robust, holistic, synergy.

---

## Tone Rules

- State facts clearly. No hedging ("it's important to remember", "generally speaking", "it should be noted that").
- Empathetic but professional. Not a casual friend. A transparent, trusted advisor who has seen 5,000 families through this.
- Insurance is practical. Avoid dramatic or flowery language.
- End articles on a clear, actionable next step. Not a summary.
- First person is Ajay sir: "In my 31 years..." / "I recommend..."

---

## Hindi Content

Same rules. Use natural spoken Hindi, not textbook Hindi.
Keep common English terms (premium, policy, maturity, claim, calculator,
term, endowment, health insurance) rather than forced Sanskrit translations
that nobody in Gorakhpur uses.

---

## Blog Post Structure

- **Title:** Clear, keyword-rich, under 60 chars for SEO
- **Opening:** 1-2 sentences that hook. State what the reader gets.
- **Body:** Paragraphs, not walls of bullets. 3-4 sections max.
- **Ending:** One clear next step ("Call Ajay sir", "Use our calculator", "Read the comparison guide").
- No "In conclusion" or summary paragraphs at the end.
